He can be a bit clumsy at times; this was especially emphasized in "Clumsy Catboy." Catboy's costume is a dark blue superhero suit with artificial fur, stripes resembling a lightning bolt, and a tail. He has darker blue accents at his feet and a cat insignia at his chest. When going underwater or on space missions, he wears his spacesuit, which includes a helmet, gloves, and boots worn over his regular superhero costume. As of the episode "The PJ Riders," Catboy gains a Riding Armor anytime he summons Cat Stripe King. The armor gives him a helmet with blue goggles, additional armored plates that cover his usual superhero costume, and glowing blue stripes. The one time he became Night Panther, his appearance is identical to that of his superhero identity, but his different color is black with white stripes and dark gray eyes.

The PJ Riders are a group of animals which represent the PJ Masks' animal routes in the PJ Masks television series. They are first seen as silhouettes in the Season 5 episode "Ninja Power Up" and officially made their debut in "Carly and Cartoka." Originally resided in the PJ Crystal Totem, but have been seen as spirits on occasions where the PJ Masks activated their powers, they assumed physical forms for the team to ride on by connecting with them and the PJ Pets in response to the destruction of the PJ vehicles by the Speedy Twins. Since then, they can be summoned by the PJ Masks placing their hands on their animal symbols located on their suits and reciting their own chant.

The PJ Riders have saddles on their backs and protective armor with glowing stripes. Cat Stripe King resembles a huge blue feline, Eagle Owl resembles a huge red owl, and Power Lizard resembles either a gecko-like lizard (as a spirit) or a huge crocodile, Komodo dragon, salamander, or an alligator (physical form).

Powers

  • PJ Masks Power: The PJ Riders has the same powers as the PJ Masks like the PJ Pets. The PJ Riders could do anything that the PJ Masks do.
  • Transfer Power: As revealed in "Crash Track Trick," they can also transfer their energy to one another, making the PJ Rider receiving that energy more powerful and the other one extremely exhausted until the power is given back to them.
